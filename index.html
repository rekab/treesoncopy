ABOUT

In the present-day, moody, Pacific Northwest, people go about their day like every other day, unaware of the environmental tragedies occurring around them. While some fight for the trees, others have come to accept there is no changing the status quo. Ash, heir to a logging empire, is at the center of it all and for them, life is easy. But when they are suddenly confronted with a life-changing decision, they are forced to reflect on their purpose in life: remain loyal to the firm, join the activists, or‚Äîcarve a new path of their own. As Terra, the activists, and Wind, Water and Fire guide them, they contemplate what their legacy will be. Tensions rise between the loggers and activists, local and global needs, and the present and future in this love story between humanity and Earth. Ash's journey unfolds via a soaring soundtrack blending indigenous, Western and folk music, resulting in an Americana story which demonstrates how we each have the power to act to save the world. The question is‚Ä¶ Will you play your part before it's too late?

TREESON was conceived in April 2020 and has been in development for 5 years. Most recently it had a sold-out off-Broadway concert debut at 54Below on Earth Day. It was also a Eugene O'Neill Semi-finalist (2023) and received a truncated production at The Dairy Arts Center in Boulder, Colorado (August, 2022) produced by Parlando School of Musical Arts. The Original Cast Recording (demo) was released in 2021.

CREATIVE

G. Victoria Campbell

Music, Lyrics & Book

G. Victoria Campbell, Ph.D. (she/her) is a Seattle-based industrial-organizational psychologist turned artist-activist, playwright-composer-lyricist and poet. As a multiracial female who grew up feeling in-between and othered, she leverages her multicultural heritage, time living abroad as an expat in Switzerland, and her background in organizational change management to inform her art, which focuses on driving positive global change.

She's best known for her original musical TREESON: AN ECO-MUSICAL (sold-out, off-Broadway concert '24, O'Neill Semi-finalist, '23; New Musical Festival, '22). Her other works include PHILIPPA (about Philippa Duke Schuyler with collaborator Stephanie Henry) and OFF WITH YOUR HEAD (a maternal love story about the Queen of Hearts with collaborator Nicolette Blount).

Andrew Coopman

Director & Choreographer

ANDREW COOPMAN (they/he) is a genderqueer director, choreographer, and playwright of Middle Eastern/European descent. Andrew's work has been on stages in Chicago, Dallas, Milwaukee, New York City, and throughout the Pacific Northwest.

Directing & Choreography credits include: HEAD OVER HEELS (Lakewood Playhouse), Bryony Lavery's FROZEN: A Play (UW Cabaret Theater), THE CONVENT OF PLEASURE (Ghostlight Theatre Ensemble), I & YOU (Burien Actor's Theater), and many more.

Joseph Purdue

Orchestrations

JOSEPH PURDUE is a composer and lyricist from the United Kingdom. He has written music and lyrics for three original musicals, 'Legends Of Arahma', 'Catfish' and 'A Tale of Fellowship'. His works have been performed multiple times in London and have featured many West End stars.

He also works as a freelance arranger, orchestrator and audio producer, contributing to TREESON: AN ECO-MUSICAL and FOR TONIGHT.
